Service Users and Needs

Across ESDC, a range of users would interact with and benefit from a Service Analytics Evidence Base.  As an example, if we adopt “policy as code," policy users will encode policy and make its rules available to others, who will use it digitally for service implementation, evaluation, and delivery. As one example, the W/T region makes key operations performance data available online to all the region’s employees.

Each user would interact with the SAEB in different ways, as their needs are different. The following table of user stories identifies the classes of users who would add and derive value from the SAEB and identifies their needs.

User Goal Reason
Policy Maker A means to evaluate evidence that could inform policy design ESDC produces better policy
Policy Evaluator To evaluate the implementation and performance of ESDC policies Service delivery is better aligned to policy
Policy Modeller Tools to facilitate changes in policies and their implementation Make recommendations for improvements on current implementation of policy
Business Analyst To evaluate service operations through the consideration of any relevant evidence Make evidence-based service operations recommendations for adjustments and improvements to Service Operators
Market Researcher To identify service delivery trends by relating service data, market data and models Make recommendations on service delivery in the future
Market Researcher To identify gaps in relevant data such as demographic data and those outlined in Appendix C Promotion of services can be improved by addressing target audience needs
Service Delivery Team Integrate feedback with real time performance metrics Design, deliver and continuously improve service delivery mechanisms for clients and employees
Agent Have access to relevant information, metrics and history of the client and relevant services To meet the client needs and provide a personalized service
